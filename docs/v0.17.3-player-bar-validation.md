# v0.17.3 Player Bar Validation

This validation covers the compact output and volume controls in the persistent
portal player bar.

## Behavior

- The player bar shows only the active zone name, with explicit volume-down and
  volume-up buttons directly beneath it. Each press applies exactly one native
  step without opening the panel.
- Pressing the zone name toggles the same panel open and closed. Escape and an
  outside press also close it.
- The panel uses a borderless, minimal surface with two permanently visible
  sections: Volume first and Zones second. Changing zone needs no intermediate
  disclosure or additional click.
- A single-output zone shows one set of volume controls labelled with the zone
  name. Group-level and per-output controls only coexist for grouped zones.
- Volume buttons use the `volume_down` and `volume_up` symbols, and numeric
  sliders render as bars without visible circular thumbs.
- A group step issues one native step command per output. Outputs already at
  their minimum or maximum are skipped without blocking the remaining outputs.
- Numeric outputs use `relative_step`; incremental outputs use `relative` with
  `-1` or `1`.
- Individual sliders retain absolute volume control.
- The popover is positioned above the complete player bar on desktop and mobile.

## Automated Validation

Run:

```powershell
node --check portal/app.js
pnpm run test
pnpm run build
```

Focused behavior coverage is in `test/portal-mini-player.test.js`. Portal asset
and integration assertions remain in `test/portal-server.test.js`; together they
cover the output popover resource, compact control selectors, per-output step
dispatch, group fan-out, native step modes, output limits and absolute sliders.

## Manual Validation

Validated with the local portal preview at desktop and 390 x 844 mobile sizes:

1. A single-output zone shows only one slider and one pair of step controls.
2. A simulated three-output group shows one group stepper and independent
   values and ranges for all three outputs.
3. The group increment reports that all three outputs received one step.
4. Zone switching is visible in the same open panel as volume controls.
5. Desktop and mobile keep the zone name plus both quick volume buttons visible.
6. The zone button toggles the panel closed after opening it.
7. The playback progress continues updating without rebuilding the artwork.
